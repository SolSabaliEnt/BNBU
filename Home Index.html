Welcome
Build & Boss Up Network
Forward Movement Toward Self-Determination
Build and Boss Up Network is a collective alliance of entrepreneurs, business owners, professionals, and community builders organized around one purpose: Black Self-Determination.
The Condition
The Root of the Problem
Black people in America have faced systemic barriers for generations — producing a documented condition of harm across every major area of life: economic, educational, housing, health, and governance.

At the root of vulnerability in every one of those areas is a single issue: the lack of collective economic power. Without it, there is no meaningful leverage anywhere else.

We are discontent with the notion that any external political party or government has — or ever will — truly prioritize our safety and wellbeing. Without economic power, there is nothing to compel them to.
Do you agree?
The Answer
Self-Determination is the Pathway
Because no external force will save us, we must become proactive in taking ownership of our own condition — and work collectively to build what we need to survive and thrive as a people.

This is Self-Determination. It means gaining collective control over the 5 Essential Institutions that directly affect the safety and wellbeing of our families:
EconomicEducationHousingHealthGovernance
We are not separatist or extremist. We are responsible.
Do you agree?
The Foundation
The Economic Pathway to Freedom
Of the 5 Essential Institutions, economic empowerment is the foundation. It is the pathway to real freedom in America — and the source of leverage in every political and social arena.

As it has been said: power concedes nothing without a demand — and you cannot make a demand without the power to back it up.

We are not black capitalists. We are not chasing individual wealth or consumer status. We are advocates of true Black wealth — and that means something specific.
Do you agree?
The Definition
What is Black Wealth?
True Black wealth is not an individual achievement. It is institutional and community ownership of the systems that govern daily life — businesses, property, schools, health infrastructure, and governance structures that are controlled by us and held accountable to our best interest.

This also means equipping Black families with the tools, knowledge, and networks to become economically sustainable — because collective power must be built from a foundation of household stability.
Do you agree that collective, community-level economic empowerment — not consumer capitalism — is the solution?
The Blueprint
Not Idealism. A Plan.
This kind of wealth can be practically and gradually built — block by block, village by village, community by community. We are not dreamers. We are builders with a blueprint and the discipline to execute it.

That blueprint works in 3 parts:
CreateDevelop profitable, sustainable Black-owned businesses and institutions empowered to be both successful and community-centered.
ConnectUnite those businesses into a collective network where they support, sustain, and strengthen one another.
BuildPool our resources to develop the 5 Essential Institutions, collectively owned at the community level — block by block, community by community.
Do you agree this is the path forward?

We are not looking for passive supporters or observers. We are looking for individuals who want to play an active and direct role in this initiative — people ready to build, not just believe.
